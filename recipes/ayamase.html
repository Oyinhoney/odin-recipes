AYAMASE

How To Prepare Ayamase Sauce.

Ayamase sauce, also known as Ofada stew or Designer stew, originated in Ikenne-Remo, Ogun State, Nigeria. The sauce is a creation of Mrs. Felicia Ajibabi Adesina, who began selling her stew in the town in the 1920s. The name Ayamase is said to come from the name of Mrs. Adesina's husband, Mase, and the Yoruba word "aya" which means "wife". Mrs. Adesina started selling her stew by walking around town, but eventually set up a stall in front of her home. Ayamase is a spicy sauce made with unripe habaneros, locust beans, onions, eggs, and vegetable oil. It's traditionally served with Ofada rice, a locally grown Nigerian rice with a unique aroma and slightly sticky texture. Ayamase is also known as Obe Aya Mase, Obe-iresi, and Obe dudu.

Ingredients

    Green/unripe habanero peppers
    Locust beans
    Wite onions
    vegetable oil
    cow skin/pomo
    cow ofals
    chicken bullion
    Salt

Steps

NB:WASH WITH WATER NOT SOAP

    Wash your cow ofals and transfer them into a clean pot
    Add your preferred season and spices eg rosemary,thyme,curry,bay leaves.
    Add onions and put it on medium heat to cook for 30mins-45mins
    After 30mins to 45mins check yor ofals to see if it is soft enough for you and if it is then your protein is ready
    While the ofals are on fire you should wash and rough blend your pepper and slice your white onions, Wash your locust beans,wash your cowskin/pomo
    Heat your sauce pan on the stove on high heat for 2mins to make sure no water is in it.
    When your pan is heated,reduce the fire to medium heat and pour in your vegetable oil add your sliced white onions and fry in the oil for about 3mins
    Pour your roughly blened pepper into the heated oil and let it cook fro about 5mins
    Add your broth you got from the ofals(little broth)
    Add yor seasoning(chicken bullion), add yor locust beans,add your pomo,add the ofals you cooked.
    let to cook for 10-15mins then serve with rice.
